Scales Eval Judge Cheatsheet

Pro Prelim Round

Note: These categories are mostly presented in 2 pt intervals. Give the higher number if the player displays the noted characteristics, but at a higher level than standard.

Scoring Categories

Rareness

  • 0: Player is only doing named tricks known by most skilled players.

  • 1-3: Player does a few unique tricks, but their tricks are predominantly named or have been seen before.

  • 4-5: Player begins to differentiate themself from other players by doing several unique tricks or displaying their own personal style.

  • 6-7: Player does a significant number of unique tricks, and displays a routine that is out of the norm in some significant way.

  • 8-9: Player is doing mostly unique tricks, and a named trick is rare. Their routine is very unique and speaks to their own style of playing yoyo.

  • 10: Player performs almost 100% unique, non-named tricks and/or creates a routine that is so far outside the box that it blows the judges away.

Zoning

  • 0: The player only does tricks in one area (i.e. in front of their body) and doesn't move.

  • 1-3: The player displays yoyo tricks in 1-2 areas (i.e. they do tricks in front of them and to their side.) and shows some movement.

  • 4-5: The player begins to show skill in using the position of the yoyo to best display tricks. They may also use some movement to display tricks appropriately.

  • 6-7: The player displays tricks that use a number of areas around their body effectively. They may also move further away from the camera to show bigger tricks, or closer for more technical ones.

  • 8-9: The player utilizes areas around their body very skillfully, using the position of the yoyo to display tricks in a way not otherwise possible. Their body movements are well thought-out and executed well.

  • 10: The player expertly utilizes the zones around their body to display tricks in unforseen ways. Their ability to be in the right part of the frame at the right time is second-to-none.

Musicality

  • 0: The routine has no noticable music cues. The player shows no coordination with their music.

  • 1-3: The routine has some coordination with the music, but not many noticable music cues.

  • 4-5: The routine is generally coordinated with the music, with a few useful music cues.

  • 6-7: The rouine's coordination with the music is significant to the routine. There are a few impactful moments of the routine having to do with music cues or coordination.

  • 8-9: The routine's coordination with music is exceptional, with a marked number of music cues and a high level of coordination. The music may be important in telling some kind of story.

  • 10: The routine's music coordination is near perfect, and all music cues are used in an effective way. It is as if the music selected was made for this routine.
